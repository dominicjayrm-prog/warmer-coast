## 🇬🇮 Gibraltar — the big 2026 change

### UK-EU Treaty on Gibraltar (the "border deal")

- **Status:** Draft text published 26 February 2026. EU Council of
  Permanent Representatives (Coreper) greenlit the agreement on 1 April
  2026.
- **Implementation:** **Provisional application from 15 July 2026.**
- **What it does:**
  - Removes the physical land border between Gibraltar and Spain.
  - Removes the fence at the La Línea crossing.
  - Schengen border rules apply at Gibraltar's port and airport.
  - Customs union established between EU and Gibraltar.
  - EES (Entry/Exit System) will **not** apply to Gibraltar residents
    at the land frontier in the meantime.
- **Sovereignty:** UK sovereignty over Gibraltar unchanged.
- **Frontier workers:** Massive practical win — no more 90/180-day
  Schengen day-counting issues, no EES queues, much faster crossings.

Sources:
- [House of Commons Library briefing](https://commonslibrary.parliament.uk/research-briefings/cbp-10572/)
- [EU Council press release 1 April 2026](https://www.consilium.europa.eu/en/press/press-releases/2026/04/01/eu-uk-relations-member-states-greenlight-eu-uk-deal-on-gibraltar/)
- [GBC: Treaty text published](https://www.gbc.gi/news/treaty-text-published)

### Cat 2 (Category 2 Individual) — high-net-worth residency

- **Net worth requirement:** £2,000,000.
- **Approved accommodation in Gibraltar:** Required.
- **Tax:** Worldwide income capped at first £118,000 assessable. Minimum
  annual tax £37,000 ish, maximum around £44,740.
- **Application:** Filed via the Finance Centre Director, vetting takes
  3-6 months typically.

### HEPSS (High Executive Possessing Specialist Skills)

- **For:** Senior specialists recruited into a Gibraltar role where no
  suitable local candidate exists.
- **Income basis:** Cap on assessable income at approximately £160,000.
- **Effective tax ceiling:** ~£44,000 per year.
- **Employer-driven:** Employer files on behalf of the candidate.

## 🇪🇸 Spain — 2026 figures

### Digital Nomad Visa (DNV)

- **Income threshold:** **€2,849 per month** (200% of the Spanish SMI).
- **Dependent uplift:** +75% IPREM per dependent (~€480/month each).
- **Savings requirement:** Generally none beyond the income, but
  consulates may ask for proof of 100% of annual income equivalent.
- **Maximum Spanish client income (self-employed):** 20% of total income.
- **Beckham Law eligible:** Yes — the DNV is the structural bridge to
  Beckham Law for most British remote workers.

### Non-Lucrative Visa (NLV)

- **Income required:** Approximately €2,400 per month for single
  applicant (tied to IPREM index, 400% of IPREM annually = ~€28,800 per
  year). Updated annually.
- **Work prohibited** in or for Spain. Remote work for non-Spanish
  employers is technically prohibited under NLV — use the DNV instead.

### Beckham Law (Régimen impatriado)

- **Tax rate:** **24% flat** on Spanish-sourced employment income up to
  €600,000 per year. 47% above the cap.
- **Duration:** Year of arrival plus 5 subsequent tax years (6 total).
- **Eligibility:** Must not have been a Spanish tax resident in any of
  the 5 tax years before the move.
- **Election deadline:** **Modelo 149 must be filed within 6 months of
  Social Security registration.** This deadline is absolute. Miss it
  and you lose Beckham Law eligibility permanently for this stay.
- **2023 reforms expanded eligibility** to remote workers (via DNV) and
  certain qualifying self-employed in entrepreneurial activities.

### IPREM (the multiplier behind most thresholds)

- **2026 IPREM:** €600 per month, €7,200 per year (12 monthly payments)
  or €8,400 per year (14 payments depending on context).

### Modelo 720 (foreign asset declaration)

- **Reporting threshold:** €50,000 per asset category (bank accounts,
  securities, real estate).
- **Subsequent years:** Required only when value rises by €20,000+ in
  any category.
- **Deadline:** 31 March of the year following the relevant tax year.
- **Post-2022 ECJ ruling:** Penalties now proportionate to standard
  Spanish tax penalty rules, not the previous 150% minimum.

### CGT on UK property after Spain move

- Sell while UK resident: UK CGT applies, principal residence relief
  usually shelters the gain.
- Sell after Spanish residency triggers: Spain has primary taxing
  rights at 19-28%, with no equivalent to UK PRR.

### UK-Spain double tax treaty

- **Government pensions** (NHS, civil service, teachers, armed forces):
  Taxed in the UK only, not in Spain.
- **State pension + private/occupational pensions:** Taxed in Spain
  only once you are Spanish resident.

## 🇵🇹 Portugal — 2026 figures

### IFICI / NHR 2.0 (the replacement regime)

- **Status of old NHR:** Closed to new applicants. Existing NHR
  beneficiaries retain their 10-year status.
- **IFICI tax rate:** **20% flat** on qualifying Portuguese-source
  employment (Category A) or self-employment (Category B) income.
- **Foreign income:** Most foreign-source income is exempt from
  Portuguese taxation under IFICI, though it counts for rate
  determination.
- **Qualifying activities (the narrower list):**
  - Technology: software developers, data scientists, IT architects,
    cybersecurity, AI / ML engineers.
  - Research & Science: research scientists, biotechnology,
    pharmaceutical R&D, academic researchers.
  - Engineering: aerospace, industrial, electrical, mechanical
    engineers in innovation / R&D roles.
  - Education: university professors, researchers, education tech.
  - Qualified professionals in strategic sectors, startup ecosystem.
- **Eligibility gates:**
  - No Portuguese tax residency in the previous 5 years.
  - Never used the original NHR or other Portuguese tax incentives.
  - Must establish Portuguese tax residency **after 1 January 2024**.
  - Provide academic qualifications or at least 3 years of professional
    experience.

### D7 Visa (passive income / retiree route)

- **Minimum income:** **€820 per month** (€9,840 per year).
- **Source:** Passive income — pensions, dividends, rental, royalties.
- **Suits:** Retirees, FIRE applicants, anyone with substantial passive
  income.

### D8 Visa (digital nomad)

- **Minimum income:** **€3,680 per month** (4× Portuguese minimum wage
  in 2026, which is €920).
- **Savings:** At least €11,040 (12 × minimum wage).
- **Suits:** Remote employees of non-Portuguese companies, qualifying
  self-employed remote workers.

### Golden Visa

- **Status:** Residential property no longer qualifies (since October
  2023).
- **What still qualifies:** Fund investment (€500,000+), capital
  transfer routes, research / arts donations.
- **Most British movers:** Use D7 or D8 instead.

### IRS (Portuguese income tax)

- **Bracket structure (2026):** Progressive from 13% to 48% across
  9 bands. Top band kicks in over ~€83,696.
- **IFICI flat rate:** Overrides IRS for qualifying activities at 20%.

### UK pension treatment under DTA

- **Government pensions:** Taxed in UK only.
- **Private and state pensions:** Taxed in Portugal once Portuguese
  resident. Under old NHR these had a 10-year exemption; under IFICI
  they do not.

## 🇬🇧 UK side (always-applicable to British movers)

### Form P85

- Notify HMRC of departure within 90 days of leaving.
- Required to claim split-year treatment in the year of move.

### UK ISA treatment after leaving

- ISA wrapper is not recognised in Spain or Portugal — income and gains
  inside the wrapper become taxable in the new country.
- Most movers crystallise and close UK ISAs before becoming foreign
  resident.

### UK CGT on principal residence

- Sell while UK resident: Principal Residence Relief usually fully
  shelters the gain.
- Sell after foreign tax residency triggers: Foreign country has
  primary taxing rights, no PRR equivalent.

buyer module, the workflow is:

1. Cross-reference with the primary government source (gov.uk,
   sede.agenciatributaria.gob.es, portaldasfinancas.gov.pt,

   fiscal, advogado, Cat 2 specialist) for the current interpretation.

date, and tell the reader to verify.
